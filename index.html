Welcome! My name is Hyeonsam Tak (you may call me Sam). I received my B.A. in Russian and Economics from Hankuk University of Foreign Studies in 2024. I am currently a research assistant in the Office of Macroeconomic Analysis and Forecasting at the Korea Development Institute (KDI).

I study the intersection of political psychology, social psychology, sociology, and economics. Specifically, I focus on the following research questions: (1) How do social structures alter the effects of personal attributes, such as socioeconomic status, on outgroup exclusion, moral reasoning, and prosocial behavior? (2) How do variables representing social trust and social integration at the national level influence suicide? (3) How do cultural characteristics influence individual behavior?

I primarily use multi-method approaches with international samples, but I am also interested in empirical analysis using methodologies from applied macroeconomics, such as cross-country panel analysis, the synthetic control method, and large Bayesian VAR models.
