Economist and Researcher

I am a researcher specializing in the intersection of economics and social psychology. My work employs quantitative methods to investigate how socioeconomic factors shape individual behavior, prosociality, and key societal outcomes.

About Me

I hold a Bachelor of Arts in Russian and Economics from Hankuk University of Foreign Studies. My research agenda is driven by a desire to understand complex social issues through a quantitative lens. My recent work explores topics such as the relationship between suicide rates and internalized morality, the dynamics of outgroup exclusion based on income congruence, and the factors influencing prosocial civic engagement.

Currently, I am a Research Assistant in the Office of Macroeconomic Analysis and Forecasting at the Korea Development Institute (KDI), where I contribute to key national economic reports, including the KDI Economic Outlook and Monthly Economic Trends. My professional experience also includes roles at the Korea Institute for Industrial Economics and Trade (KIET) and the Ministry of SMEs and Startups.

My technical skills include proficiency in Stata, R, Python, and Mplus. I am a native Korean speaker, fluent in English, and have an intermediate proficiency in Russian.
